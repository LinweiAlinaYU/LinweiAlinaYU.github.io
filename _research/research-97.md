Mathematical Contest in Modeling and Interdisciplinary Contest in Modeling **(MCM/ICM)** are annual international competitions that challenge undergraduate and graduate students to solve real-world problems using mathematical modeling and interdisciplinary approaches. The contests are organized by the Consortium for Mathematics and Its Applications (COMAP) and attract participants from universities and colleges worldwide. And our team's job is **to develop a Risk Assessment Model for Light Pollution in three days and complete a 24-page thesis.**  

As the **team leader**, my responsibilities include designing mathematical models, sharing my thoughts with team members, welcoming their input, and assigning specific tasks to team members. 
<br/><img src='/images/MCM-2.png' style='width: 100%;'>

We innovatively use **Gaussian mixture model** and **EM algorithm** to fit every city on the light pollution map. What's more, we firstly integrate the **optical flow estimation**
algorithm to quantify the spatial change of light pollution.  
<br/><img src='/images/MCM-3.png' style='width: 50%;'><img src='/images/MCM-4.png' style='width: 50%;'>

We also use **some indirect methods** to evaluate light pollution. We assess light pollution in a certain area by considering the changes in bird population and the impact of light exposure on human melatonin secretion. We fit data curves to evaluate the level of light pollution in the area.  
<br/><img src='/images/MCM-5.png' style='width: 100%;'>

We visualize the impact of our four indicators and prove that our indicators are efficient.
