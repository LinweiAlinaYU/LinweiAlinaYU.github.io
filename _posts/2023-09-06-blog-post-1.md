This is a communication-related popular science video that was created as a requirement for the Communication Principles (challenging course). It is also an inspiration that emerged from my study of machine learning algorithms combined with the knowledge of QPSK modulation and demodulation learned in the Communication Principles course.

In an Additive White Gaussian Noise (AWGN) channel, when performing QPSK modulation at the transmitter, the receiver will observe **a cloud of four uniformly distributed Gaussian points**. In this scenario, using a uniformly partitioned constellation diagram for decision-making yields the best results. However, if the channel **is not an AWGN channel and exhibits "discrimination" towards different symbols**, the four Gaussian distributions at the receiver will be different. At this point, if there is a machine learning algorithm that can **automatically fit the means and variances of these four Gaussian distributions**, and perform a nonlinear segmentation of the constellation diagram based on probabilities for decision-making, the bit error rate can be significantly reduced.
